Leadership

Catherine Huang
Founder and Executive Director
Catherine Huang, from San Jose, CA, studies with Hans Boepple. Passionate about musical outreach, she kickstarted H.O.P.E. to unite pianists and audiences across the world amidst these trying times. Additionally, she is the Vice President of Lynbrook High School Music for Community, organized a benefit concert raising over $4,000 to aid California Wildfires victims, and has taught keyboard for free to underprivileged students. Catherine also enjoys dance, coding, Spanish, and exploring the outdoors. She is a rising freshman at Harvard College.
Benjamin T. Rossen
Executive Director and Media Coordinator
A Young Steinway Artist and Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center guest artist, Benjamin T. Rossen, Great Neck, NY, is a multifaceted musician. He is a pianist, composer, and French hornist. He graduated from the Manhattan School of Music Precollege Division in 2020 under Professor Jeffrey Cohen and Professor Sharon Moe as a double major candidate in piano and French horn. In addition to H.O.P.E., Benjamin was the founder of the Music Creators Society, an extracurricular program that facilitates collaboration between artists, composers, and writers to produce interdisciplinary works. In the fall of 2020, Benjamin will be attending Harvard University and the New England Conservatory through the A.B./M.M. five-year program.
Josephine Chou
Executive Director
Josephine Chou, a rising senior from Saratoga, California, studies with Hans Boepple. She is the president of her school’s Tri-M, a club that aims to unite students in their love of music, bring students’ musical talents to community centers, and partner with other Bay Area schools to raise money for causes. In addition to music, she is an avid writer who has had her poems published in literary journals. She also enjoys baking and hiking.
